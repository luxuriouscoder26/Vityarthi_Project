# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and banking details. It is one of the most common and widespread online threats.

A typical phishing attack occurs in several steps. First, the attacker sends an email or message that appears to be from a trusted source, such as a bank, online service, or educational institution. The message often includes a sense of urgency, such as warning about account suspension or offering a reward. Next, the victim is asked to click on a link that redirects them to a fake website designed to look identical to the original. When the victim enters their login credentials or personal details, the attacker captures this information. In some cases, malicious attachments are also used to install harmful software on the victim’s device.

Phishing attacks usually target students, employees, and individuals who frequently use online services but may not carefully verify email authenticity.

The consequences can be severe. Victims may experience unauthorized access to their accounts, financial loss, identity theft, and data breaches. Organizations may also suffer reputational damage and loss of customer trust.

This case study emphasizes the importance of being cautious online. Users should verify the sender’s email address, avoid clicking on suspicious links, and enable security features like two-factor authentication to protect their accounts.
