# Responsible Social Media Use: A College Student's Checklist
 
## ✅ DO's
 
| # | Do |
|---|-----|
| 1 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself if the content is something you'd be comfortable with a professor, employer, or family member seeing. |
| 2 | **Keep your privacy settings updated** – Regularly review who can see your posts and personal information on each platform. |
| 3 | **Give credit where it's due** – Always attribute quotes, images, or ideas to their original creators to respect intellectual property. |
| 4 | **Be respectful and constructive** – Engage in discussions with empathy; disagree with ideas politely rather than attacking individuals. |
| 5 | **Fact-check before sharing** – Verify information from credible sources before reposting news, statistics, or claims. |
 
---
 
## ❌ DON'Ts
 
| # | Don't |
|---|-------|
| 1 | **Don't overshare personal information** – Avoid posting your location in real time, phone number, home address, or daily schedule publicly. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or harassment** – Never post hateful, demeaning, or threatening content about others online. |
| 3 | **Don't plagiarize or misrepresent content** – Do not pass off others' work as your own or spread misleading information. |
| 4 | **Don't post under the influence** – Avoid posting when emotionally charged or impaired, as impulsive posts can have long-lasting consequences. |
| 5 | **Don't ignore red flags from strangers** – Be cautious about accepting friend requests or engaging with unknown accounts that seem suspicious. |
 
---
 
*Remember: Your digital footprint is permanent. Practice mindful social media habits to protect your reputation and well-being.*
